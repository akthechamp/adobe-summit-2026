### Task 11: Elevator Pitch & Conversation Strategy (Day 10 — April 15, 1 hr)

**Why:** You'll have 30-60 seconds to make an impression in most interactions. You need 3 versions of your pitch.

- [ ] **Step 1: Craft your pitches (30 min)**

  **The 10-Second Version (for "what do you do?"):**
  ```
  "I run a 20-person development agency. We specialize in Adobe Commerce
  and marketing automation — building stores, migrating platforms, and
  connecting everything from Marketo to Salesforce."
  ```

  **The 30-Second Version (for interested follow-up):**
  ```
  "We're a development agency of about 20 people — developers and designers.
  Our sweet spot is Adobe Commerce — I've been building on Magento since before
  the Adobe acquisition — plus marketing automation with Marketo and HubSpot.

  We do a lot of migration work — companies moving from Shopify to Adobe Commerce,
  or from HubSpot to Marketo — and custom development for companies that need
  something their out-of-the-box setup can't handle.

  What brings you to Summit?"
  ```

  **The Problem-Solver Version (when someone mentions a pain point):**
  ```
  "That's actually exactly what we help with. We've been doing [specific thing]
  for [type of company]. Would it make sense to grab 15 minutes this week or
  set up a call after Summit to walk through how we'd approach it?"
  ```

  **Key principle:** ALWAYS end with a question back to them. People love talking about their own challenges.

- [ ] **Step 2: Prepare conversation starters for each context (15 min)**

  | Context | Opener |
  |---------|--------|
  | In a session | "What part of this are you most interested in for your team?" |
  | At a reception/mixer | "First Summit? What's your focus area?" |
  | At Community Pavilion | "Are you exploring [product X] or already using it?" |
  | Near a sponsor booth | "Have you worked with [sponsor name] before?" |
  | After a keynote | "What was your biggest takeaway from that?" |

- [ ] **Step 3: Practice your pitch out loud (15 min)**

  Literally stand in front of a mirror or tell your friend on the trip. Practice until it sounds NATURAL, not rehearsed. You should be able to deliver it while distracted.
