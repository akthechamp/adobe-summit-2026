## Root cause — not a tier issue, a product-familiarity issue

Unlike the AEM and RTCDP fails, this was the correct tier (Professional). The gap here is different: **Workfront Project Manager tests the specific Workfront product workflows** — Issues, Requests, Custom Forms, Approval Paths, Project Templates, Portfolios, Programs, Timesheets, Resource Planning, Proofing, Reports in Workfront, Workfront Fusion integrations. It's conceptual but product-specific, not transferable from generic PM experience.

Your agency operations knowledge (running a 20-person team) gives you PM intuition, but Workfront-specific object models and navigation tests were the gap.

**Section 2 (Execute) — 46%.** Task dependencies, milestone types, predecessors, durations, timelines, status updates, issue conversion. Half of this is general PM, half is Workfront-specific. Landed at ~50%.
